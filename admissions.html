Eligibility Criteria

Intermediate Programs:

Minimum 60% marks in Matriculation or equivalent

Minimum 50% marks in Intermediate or equivalent

Master Programs:

Minimum 45% marks in Bachelor degree in relevant field

Required Documents

  • Completed application form
  • Attested copies of academic certificates and transcripts
  • Character certificate from previous institution
  • Copy of CNIC/B-Form
  • 4 recent passport-sized photographs
  • Domicile certificate
  • Migration certificate (if applicable)

Scholarships & Financial Aid

Merit Scholarship

Students with outstanding academic performance can receive up to 100% fee waiver based on their previous academic records and entrance test performance.

Need-Based Financial Aid

Financial assistance for deserving students from low-income backgrounds. Application requires proof of financial status and academic potential.

Sports Scholarship

Students with exceptional sports achievements at district, provincial, or national level can apply for sports scholarships covering up to 75% of tuition fees.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the admission process?

The admission process involves completing an application form, taking an entrance test, attending an interview, and submitting all required documents. The final selection is made based on merit.

Is there an entrance test for all programs?

Yes, all programs require an entrance test except for candidates with exceptional academic records who may be eligible for direct admission based on merit.

How can I apply for a scholarship?

Scholarship applications are available at the time of admission. You can submit the scholarship form along with your admission application with supporting documents like income certificate, academic achievements, etc.

Can I apply for multiple programs?

Yes, you can apply for up to three programs in order of preference. You will need to indicate your preferences in the application form.
